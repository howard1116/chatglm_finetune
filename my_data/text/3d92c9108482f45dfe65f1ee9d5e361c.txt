Abstract ; Waterborne polyurethanes (WPUs) are prepared with poly (1,4butanediol adi
pate)diol (PBA,M,=2 O0O) as soft segments and hydroquinone dihydroxyethyl ether (HQEE) as
chain extender. The effect of the HQEE contents on the properties of waterborne polyure-
thanes are investigated. The morphology of the emulsion is characterized by laser scattering
particle size analyzer, The structure of the films is characterized by Fourier transform infrared
spectroscopy (FTIR) and Xray diffraction (XRD). The thermal and mechanical properties
of the films are studied by the differential scanning calorimetry (DSC), thermal gravimetric
analysis (TGA)and electronic tensile testing. The results show that the content of HQEE re-
sults in the decrease of crystallinity of WPUs. Moreover, the average particle size of the emul-
sion first increases and then decreases, and the water resistance of the film decreases, and the
heat resistance of the film increases and the tensile strength of the film also increases first
and then decreases. The results indicate that the tensile strength of the film reaches up to a
maximum value of 29.9 MPa when the HQEE content is 6.93%,
Key Words: waterborne polyurethane; HOEE; crystallization; heat resistance
